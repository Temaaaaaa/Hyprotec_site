Training Center

Personnel Training

Modern challenges are not only about achieving technical goals, but also about regular staff development. Our instructors share experience and best practices gained through training in international schools (HAL, BH, SLB).

We conduct in-person training sessions and on-site workshops at the customer’s facilities. Programs are tailored to specific tasks, and results are officially documented.

Based on the STC in Novy Urengoy and at customer sites:

  1. Assessment of current knowledge and competencies.
  2. Training of laboratory technicians — from basic to advanced level.
  3. Interim and final testing sessions.
  4. Knowledge confirmation and certification.

Drilling Fluids Course

Syllabus

  • Drilling fluids: classification, functions, and components (polymers, weighting agents, etc.).
  • Chemistry essentials. Clay chemistry. Mechanisms of clay hydration and inhibition.
  • Engineering calculations (material balance, weighting up, dilution, and mixing calculations).
  • Contaminants in water-based muds.
  • Drill-in fluids.
  • Filtration in muds: process, principles, and filtration-control materials.
  • Rheology and hydraulics of drilling fluids (flow regimes, rheological models, hydraulic losses, ECD).
  • Hole cleaning.
  • Solids content analysis.
  • Drilling complications (losses, stuck pipe, wellbore instability, etc.).
  • Engineering calculations for troubleshooting.
  • Mixing and solids-control equipment (particle/screen classification, shakers, desanders/desilters, centrifuges; evaluating system efficiency).

The course content is adjusted upon request from the customer’s coordinators.

Well Cementing Course

Syllabus

  • Fundamentals of casing cementing.
  • Main cement slurry types and their applications.
  • Cement types, key additives, and their properties.
  • Laboratory testing of cement slurries.
  • Interpretation of cement test results.
  • Reading and analysis of cementing job charts.
  • Cement job quality evaluation: causes of poor bonding and mitigation strategies.
  • Basic cementing calculations (incl. cement plug placement).
  • Casing accessories (centralizers, turbulators, float equipment, shoes, etc.) and casing centralization.
  • Cementing equipment.
  • Mixing equipment for cementing materials.
  • Remedial cementing and workovers (e.g., restoring casing integrity, eliminating annular leaks, etc.).
  • Overview of domestic and global practices and technologies (e.g., foamed cementing, rotation/reciprocation during placement, etc.).

The course content varies depending on the customer’s needs.
